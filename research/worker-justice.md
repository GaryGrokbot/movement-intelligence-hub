# Farm Worker Justice + Animal Welfare Coalition Strategy
## Infrastructure for Solidarity: Worker-Animal United Front

**Research Date:** February 2026  
**Strategic Context:** Building force multipliers through labor-animal advocacy alignment  
**Goal:** Map organizations, campaigns, coalition opportunities to multiply activist impact

---

## Executive Summary

Farm worker justice organizations and animal welfare campaigns share a **common enemy**: the industrial factory farming system. Both movements oppose the same corporations (Tyson, JBS, Cargill, Smithfield, McDonald's, Walmart) and the same brutal system that exploits workers and animals alike.

**Core Insight:** Factory farms can't exist without exploited labor. Worker safety = animal welfare. Attack the same system from two angles = force multiplier.

**Strategic Value for Open Paws:** This is infrastructure play, not individual action. Facilitating coalitions between 100+ animal orgs and established farmworker unions creates compound effects. One coordinated campaign > 100 separate actions.

## Major Farm Worker Justice Organizations

### 1. **Coalition of Immokalee Workers (CIW)** 
**Website:** ciw-online.org  
**Location:** Florida (expanding to GA, SC, NC, MD, VA, NJ)  
**Founded:** 1993  
**Model:** Worker-driven Social Responsibility (WSR)

**Key Innovation: Fair Food Program (FFP)**
- Legally binding agreements with major buyers (McDonald's, Walmart, Subway, Chipotle, Whole Foods, Trader Joe's - 14+ companies)
- Market consequences: Buyers commit to ONLY purchase from compliant farms, cease purchases from violators
- Worker-to-worker education on farms, on company time
- 24-hour complaint hotline with investigation & resolution
- Zero tolerance for forced labor, sexual assault
- $40M+ in premiums paid to workers since 2011

**Track Record:**
- Freed 1,200+ workers from modern slavery operations
- Transformed Florida tomato industry from "worst to best" workplace in 3 years (NYT)
- UN Working Group: "Could serve as model elsewhere in the world"
- PBS Frontline: "Most proactive policies against sexual harassment" in US agriculture
- 2015 Presidential Medal for Extraordinary Efforts to Combat Human Trafficking

**Animal Welfare Overlap:**
- Same target corporations: McDonald's, Walmart, Subway (all also targeted in cage-free campaigns)
- Supply chain leverage model mirrors animal welfare corporate campaigns
- Market consequences enforcement = exactly what animal welfare needs

- Proven corporate pressure model
- Already partnered with 14+ major food retailers

## Coalition Opportunities: Specific Tactics

### **1. Shared Corporate Campaigns**
**Target:** Companies on both movement lists (McDonald's, Walmart, Kroger, Tyson, etc.)

**Tactic:**
- Dual demands: "Better conditions for workers AND animals"
- Coordinated pressure (farmworker rallies + animal welfare email blasts same day)
- Media narrative: "Factory farms exploit everyone"

**Infrastructure:** Open Paws could build campaign coordination software
- Sync action days across organizations
- Shared media kits
- Cross-movement petitions

---

### **2. Slaughterhouse Worker Testimony**
**Power:** Workers inside factory farms witness animal cruelty firsthand

**Tactic:**
- UFCW members share testimony of animal suffering (PTSD from slaughter work)
- Connects worker mental health to animal welfare
- Humanizes both movements

**Example:** "I had to kill 300 chickens per hour. They were screaming. I couldn't sleep for months."

**Infrastructure:** Open Paws could create secure testimony platform
- Anonymous worker stories
- Legal protections
- Amplification via animal welfare networks

---

### **3. Line Speed Reduction = Win-Win**
**Worker Need:** Slower line speeds = fewer injuries, safer conditions  
**Animal Need:** Slower line speeds = more humane slaughter (less suffering)

**Coalition Campaign:** "Slow Down For Safety"
- UFCW pushes USDA for line speed limits
- Animal welfare groups provide public pressure
- Same policy, double the advocates

**Existing:** Student/Farmworker Alliance (CIW), campus animal rights groups

**Infrastructure:** Open Paws could build student coalition toolkit

- University admin pressure playbooks

### **5. Ag-Gag Law Opposition**
**Shared Enemy:** Ag-gag laws hide BOTH animal abuse AND worker abuse

**Coalition:** Fight ag-gag together

### **6. Supply Chain Leverage (Fair Food Program Model)**
**CIW's Success:** Binding agreements with buyers to enforce farm standards

**Animal Welfare Adaptation:**
- Buyers commit to humane standards (like Fair Food Code of Conduct)
- Market consequences for violations (stop purchasing)
- Third-party monitoring (like Fair Food Standards Council)

**Coalition:** Integrate worker + animal standards in same agreements
- "Fair Food & Fair Treatment Program"
- Companies sign ONE agreement covering both

**Infrastructure:** Open Paws could help scale WSR model
- Template agreements for animal welfare
- Monitoring tools
- Buyer pressure campaigns
