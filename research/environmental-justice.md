## Key Connections Identified

### 1. POLLUTION & HEALTH DISPARITIES
**The Issue:**
- CAFOs (Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations) concentrate in low-income communities and communities of color
- Air pollution: ammonia, hydrogen sulfide, particulate matter
- Water contamination: manure lagoons, nitrate runoff
- Health impacts: respiratory disease, cancer clusters, mental health impacts

### 2. CLIMATE JUSTICE
**The Issue:**
- Factory farming = major greenhouse gas source (methane, nitrous oxide)
- Climate impacts disproportionately harm marginalized communities
- False solutions (like biogas from manure) extend pollution
- Agricultural emissions concentrated in environmental justice communities

### 3. WATER CONTAMINATION
**The Issue:**
- Manure runoff → algae blooms, dead zones
- Nitrate contamination of drinking water
- Disproportionate impact on rural poor, Indigenous communities
- Waterways serving marginalized communities polluted first, cleaned last

### 4. LAND RIGHTS & SOVEREIGNTY
**The Issue:**
- CAFO expansion on Indigenous lands
- Displacement of small farmers, particularly Black and Latino farmers
- Loss of traditional foodways and sovereignty
- Corporate agriculture as colonialism continuation

## Strategic Framework: Environmental Justice × Animal Protection

### Shared Goals
1. **Shut down/limit CAFOs** - reduces both animal suffering AND community pollution
2. **Corporate accountability** - Tyson, JBS, Smithfield harm both animals and communities
3. **Just transition** - away from industrial animal agriculture
4. **Food sovereignty** - community-controlled, plant-forward food systems

### Campaign Overlaps with Current Open Paws Work

#### Restaurant Chain Pressure Campaign
**EJ Angle:** 
- McDonald's, KFC, Subway source from CAFOs polluting marginalized communities
- Cage eggs → CAFOs → pollution hotspots
- Frame: "Your cheap egg comes at the cost of Black and Brown communities' health"

**EJ Angle:**
- Tyson, JBS, Cargill, Smithfield hide BOTH animal suffering AND pollution data
